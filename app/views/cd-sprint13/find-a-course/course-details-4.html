Who is this course for

A Level English Literature is part of the Humanities A level pathway. Course: Love Through the Ages: Othello, Unseen Poetry, The Great Gatsby, Pre-1900 Love Poetry Texts in Shared Contexts: A Streetcar Named Desire, Unseen Prose, The Handmaid’s Tale, Feminine Gospels Independent critical study: texts across time A 2500 word essay comparing two texts (one text must be pre-1900) English Literature encourages students to explore the relationships that exist between texts and the contexts within which they are written, received and understood. Studying texts within a shared context enables students to investigate and connect them, drawing out patterns of similarity and difference using a variety of reading strategies and perspectives. English Literature A privileges the process of making autonomous meaning, encouraging students to debate and challenge the interpretations of other readers as they develop their own informed personal responses.

Entry requirements

5 GCSEs at 4 or above including: English at 6 or above and Mathematics at 4 or above

What you'll learn

You’ll cover topics including: (bullet list) child development from conception to seven years children’s physical and emotional needs the principles of safeguarding child protection supporting children with additional needs caring for children who are ill You’ll explore your role in preparing children for starting school, investigate the concept of play and learn how to support the development of childrens' literacy and numeracy. You’ll look at perspectives on childcare and education from beyond the UK and find out more about the professionals that work in partnership in early years work. You’ll be able to go on work experience placements to get an insight into the opportunities available to you.
