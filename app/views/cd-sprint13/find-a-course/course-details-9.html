Who this course is for

If you want to become a technician or trainee manager in the construction sector, or go to university to study for professional qualifications such as architecture, civil or structural engineering or quantity surveying, this work related course could be for you. It's equivalent to three A Levels and suits people who prefer continuous assessment and coursework to final exam.

Entry requirements

4-5 GCSEs including English, maths and science at grade 4 (C) or above or an equivalent Level 2 qualification.

What you'll learn

You’ll cover topics including: (bullet list) materials and techniques used in the building industry how forces act on structures using maths to solve construction problems communicating building design in drawings, sections and specifications carrying out site surveys You’ll get project management skills such as costing construction works and monitoring progress against a budget. You’ll learn more about the construction sector, its safety practices and sustainable construction techniques. You’ll have the opportunity to take additional optional units from a varied range of topics.

How you'll learn

Through a mixture of classroom, design workshop and construction site activities.

What you'll need to bring

A scientific calculator, drawing equipment and safety boots for site visits.

How you'll be assessed

Through coursework, group and individual assignments, presentations and end of unit practical and written tests.

Next steps

You could go on to an HNC / HND, foundation degree or degree in construction related subjects. You could get a technician level job or apply for construction higher or degree apprenticeships.
