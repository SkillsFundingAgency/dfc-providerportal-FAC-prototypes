Who is this course for

This course is for you if you want to work with babies and young children from birth to 5 years. It will prepare you to get a job or to move on to higher education childcare courses. Working with young children is challenging so you'll need sensitivity, patience and the ability to work well with others.

Entry requirements

A minimum of 4 GCSEs grade 4 (C) or above including English and maths or a relevant Level 2 qualification.

What you'll learn

You’ll cover topics including: (bullet list) child development from conception to seven years children’s physical and emotional needs the principles of safeguarding child protection supporting children with additional needs caring for children who are ill You’ll explore your role in preparing children for starting school, investigate the concept of play and learn how to support the development of childrens' literacy and numeracy. You’ll look at perspectives on childcare and education from beyond the UK and find out more about the professionals that work in partnership in early years work. You’ll be able to go on work experience placements to get an insight into the opportunities available to you.

How you'll learn

Through a combination of classroom based lectures, presentations, workshops, discussions and practical work experience placements.

What you'll need to bring

How you'll be assessed

Through work placement observation, assignments and controlled assessment. You’ll also build a portfolio of evidence to demonstrate what you have learned.

Next steps

You could get a job such as an early years educator, pre-school worker, teaching assistant, playgroup leader, childminder or nanny. You could go on to higher education courses such as a degree/ foundation degree in childhood studies.
