Who is this course for

Anyone looking to work as a teaching assistant in nurseries, schools or special schools. You might already work or volunteer in a learning support role and want to move on in your career, or get paid employment. If so this course will give you the chance to get a recognised qualification. Administrative, catering and site staff in schools might also find this course useful for continuous professional development.

Entry requirements

A basic level of literacy, numeracy and ICT.

What you'll learn

You’ll learn about the stages of child development from birth to 19, including the influences and transitions that can impact normal development. You'll cover issues such as (bullet list) safeguarding children’s welfare, building appropriate relationships equality, diversity and inclusion (end list) You’ll learn how to communicate effectively with children and young people as well as their parents, carers and childcare professionals. You’ll learn strategies for promoting positive behaviour and supporting learning, as well as how to reflect on and improve your own and team practice in school. There are optional units that cover skills for working with specific client groups such as children with additional needs and for specific duties such as supporting assessment.

How you'll learn

Through a combination of classroom teaching and experience in the workplace. You'll be set individual and group projects and assignments, undertake research and do some independent study. There will be tutor presentations, demonstrations and guest speakers.

What you'll need to bring

You'll need an enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service clearance certificate for work experience placements.

How you'll be assessed

You'll build a portfolio of evidence which may include assignments, observations of your performance, oral and written questions, case studies, witness statements and photographic evidence.

Next steps

You could get a job or an apprenticeship in primary, secondary or special schools as a teaching assistant, learning support assistant or special needs assistant. You could move on to a level 3 qualification in supporting teaching and learning.
