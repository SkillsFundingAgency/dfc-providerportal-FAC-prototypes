Who this course is for

This course is for anyone who would like to start teaching or for those who are already teaching but need a nationally recognised qualification for their career development. It's suitable for work in post 16 education and the private training industry.

Entry requirements

You need to be 19 years or over. You should have achieved or be aiming to achieve a qualification at NVQ level 3 and/or have experience in the subject/skill you wish to teach.

What you'll learn

The course will teach you the skills to become an effective teacher or trainer including, your roles and responsibilities as a teacher, how to meet the needs of all learners awareness of equality, diversity and inclusivity facilitation skills techniques for motivating learners, planning and delivering learning one-to-one and practical skills teaching. You’ll develop an understanding of the principles and practices of assessment through exploring the types, strengths and limitations of assessment methods and how to involve learners in the process.

How you'll learn

Through lectures, presentations, discussions and role play exercises. You'll observe practical demonstrations and complete reflective writing exercises. There will be some self-study required and you'll have access to materials in a virtual learning environment.

What you'll need to bring

Check with course provider

How you'll be assessed

Through a combination of written coursework and observed teaching practice. You'll be observed delivering micro-teaching sessions to your peers on the course rather than actual students. You'll also complete a portfolio of evidence.

Next steps

Your employment prospects will be increased if you move on to the Certificate or the Diploma in Education and Training. You may be able to get a job as a teacher or trainer that will support you to gain such a qualification as you work.
