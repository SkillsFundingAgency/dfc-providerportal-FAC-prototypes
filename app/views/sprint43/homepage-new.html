Transferable skills are a set of skills that you’re good at. You can build them up over time, through work, volunteering, education and life. You can apply these skills to a range of jobs.

It’s not always easy to know your own skills and strengths. Our skills assessments can help you find out what they are, and what careers might suit you.

A skills assessment can be helpful at any stage of your career, like when you:

  • start your career
  • return to work
  • change your career

Discover your skills

In this assessment, answer questions about the things you like to do or are good at. See the types of job roles they match.

  • There are fewer than 50 short multiple-choice questions
  • The assessment takes 10 to 15 minutes to complete

After you finish this assessment, you may want to use your results to explore different career options. You can compare different careers and see what would suit you best.

Go to discover your skills and careers

Skills health check

The skills health check is a more in-depth collection of assessments. These assessments will generate a report, which can help you understand:

  • your personal strengths
  • what work-based skills you have
  • work-based skills you could develop

This information could be helpful if you're applying for a job or preparing for an interview.

Before you start the skills health check, you may want to know that:

  • there are 10 assessments
  • each assessment takes 15 to 30 minutes
  • you can take the assessments in any order you like

You must do at least one assessment to get your report. Your report will become more detailed every time you complete another assessment.
